# UPI/Online Payment Fraud Case Study: IIT Bombay Student's Rs 7.29 Lakh Loss

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud involves scammers tricking victims into transferring money via India's popular instant payment system, often through intimidation or deception.

In July 2024, a 25-year-old IIT Bombay student received a call from a fraudster posing as a Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) official. Step 1: The caller claimed 17 complaints were filed against his mobile number, threatening deactivation without a police NOC. Step 2: He transferred the call to a WhatsApp video showing a fake police officer in uniform, who demanded the student's Aadhaar and alleged money laundering involvement. Step 3: Under pressure, the student sent Rs 29,500 via UPI. Step 4: The next day, scammers imposed a "digital arrest," barring contact with others, and coerced him to share bank details, siphoning Rs 7 lakh more. They then released him, claiming safety.

College students like engineering and IIT aspirants are prime targets due to limited financial experience, active UPI use for fees/hostel payments, and trust in authority figures.

Consequences included total Rs 7.29 lakh loss, emotional distress from "arrest" fear, damaged credit, and time wasted reporting to police. Broader impacts: eroded trust in digital payments and potential legal hassles if funds traced to crimes.
